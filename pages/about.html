Social Issues We Are Solving

We want to solve the issue that “the human and financial resources needed to solve social issues are difficult to unite, optimally allocate, and expand”

Difficult to unite the resources needed to solve social issues

Prior to founding Socious Inc. I founded Discover Deep Japan Inc. in 2019 with a mission to build a more inclusive world. The biggest challenge I faced as a social entrepreneur is to secure the human and financial resources needed to realize our mission.

Unlike traditional businesses, social businesses need to collaborate with impact investors and hire employees who are passionate about the company’s mission. However, existing products like LinkedIn focuses on connecting people through skills and experience and cannot connect like-minded people through social passions. The resources to achieve the SDGs are not united because people try to solve the same social issue cannot easily collaborate with each other.

Difficult to optimally allocate the resources needed to solve social issues

Before becoming a social entrepreneur, I worked in the international humanitarian sector for about five years, working for international organizations such as the UNHCR and the ICRC. While it was an amazing experience and fulfilling work, my potential can be maximized by working in a small team as a social entrepreneur rather than working for big organizations like the UN and the Red Cross. However, many people who are passionate about realizing the SDGs are not in an environment where they can maximize their potential.

Difficult to expand the resources needed to solve social issues

The social sector workforce makes up on average 7.4 percent of the total workforce. The more people are involved in the social sector, the faster we can solve social issues. We believe that there are untapped resources, people who are interested in social issues but have not been able to take action. At Socious, we are on a mission to encourage people to take action on social change.

What we do

Our aim is to accelerate social change through the chemical reactions of encounters. We want to find solutions for situations in which it is difficult to gather, optimally allocate, and expand the resources (people, money, and information) necessary to solve social issues.

Gathering the resources needed to solve social issues - Meeting like-minded people

I founded Discover Deep Japan Inc. in 2019, a social business that aims to create a multicultural society, before I founded Socious Corporation. The biggest challenge I faced as a social entrepreneur was securing the human resources and funds necessary to solve social issues. Social business is different from ordinary business in that it requires co-creation and collaboration with people and investors who share the same mission, but existing products such as LinkedIn can provide connections in terms of skills and experience, but cannot provide encounters with like-minded people. Since people who aim to solve the same social issues cannot join forces, resources for achieving the SDGs will be dispersed.

Optimal allocation of resources needed to solve social issues - Encountering the right environment for you

Before I became a social entrepreneur, I worked for about five years in international humanitarian assistance at UNHCR and ICRC. It was very meaningful work and a valuable experience, but as a social entrepreneur, I can maximize my potential and create a greater social impact in a small organization rather than contributing to solving social issues as a member of a huge organization like the UN or Red Cross. However, many of the people involved in achieving the SDGs are not in an environment where they can maximize their potential.

Expanding the resources needed to solve social issues

Data shows that people like us who are fully committed to solving social issues account for about 7.4% of the working population, but if more people get involved in achieving the SDGs in various ways such as side jobs, pro bono work, and volunteering, social change will be accelerated. However, even if they are interested, it is difficult for them to participate in activities related to the SDGs.

Seira Yun

Founder & CEO

Seira Yun is a serial social entrepreneur with years of experience across countries in the Middle East, Africa and Asia leading teams within the ICRC, UNHCR, and social ventures. His lived experience as both a migrant and a pansexual queer drives Seira’s passion for social justice.

Seira is a full-stack developer and holds a Master in Social Innovation degree from the University of Cambridge Judge Business School. He founded Discover Deep Japan Inc. in 2019, a social venture with a mission to build a more inclusive world. To solve the issues he faced as a social entrepreneur, he founded Socious Inc. in 2021.
